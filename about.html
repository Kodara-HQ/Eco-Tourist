About UENR Eco-Tourist

A Living Ecosystem on Campus

Where Conservation Meets Curiosity

Nestled in the heart of Ghana's forested Brong-Ahafo region, the University of Energy and Natural Resources (UENR) in Sunyani is not only a hub of academic excellence—it is also home to one of Ghana's most intriguing and ecologically significant natural wonders: the UENR Bat Sanctuary.

What makes this sanctuary truly remarkable is that it coexists harmoniously with daily university life. As students and faculty walk to lectures, conduct fieldwork, or relax on campus, they share space with this vibrant colony of bats—creating a rare and beautiful fusion of education, ecology, and coexistence.

The Bat Sanctuary Story

A Hidden Gem on Campus

Located within the university's main campus, the bat sanctuary is a hidden gem—a unique natural habitat that houses thousands of fruit bats (Eidolon helvum) roosting peacefully among towering trees. These harmless, large-winged mammals form a crucial part of the ecosystem, aiding in pollination, seed dispersal, and forest regeneration.

Ecosystem Services

The fruit bats play a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of our campus and surrounding areas. They are essential pollinators for many native plant species and help disperse seeds across the landscape, contributing to forest regeneration and biodiversity.

Research Opportunities

The sanctuary serves as a living laboratory for researchers studying bat ecology, behavior, and their role in ecosystem health. Students and faculty conduct ongoing research projects that contribute to our understanding of these fascinating creatures.

UENR: Committed to Sustainability

Our Mission

Founded in 2012, the University of Energy and Natural Resources is a public institution dedicated to advancing knowledge in energy, natural resource management, and sustainable development. As part of its mission, UENR actively protects and promotes the ecological treasures on its land—including the bat sanctuary—as living laboratories for research, education, and conservation tourism.

Academic Excellence

The university offers programs in energy studies, natural resource management, environmental science, and sustainable development. Our faculty and students are actively engaged in research that addresses global environmental challenges while promoting local conservation efforts.

Community Engagement

UENR is committed to community engagement and environmental education. The bat sanctuary serves as an educational resource for local schools, community groups, and visitors interested in learning about conservation and biodiversity.

Our Conservation Values

Biodiversity Protection

We are committed to protecting and preserving the rich biodiversity of our campus ecosystem, including the unique bat population that calls our sanctuary home.

Education & Research

Our sanctuary serves as a living classroom where students, researchers, and visitors can learn about ecology, conservation, and the importance of biodiversity.

Community Partnership

We work closely with local communities, schools, and organizations to promote environmental awareness and conservation education.

Sustainable Tourism

We promote responsible eco-tourism that supports conservation efforts while providing educational and recreational opportunities for visitors.
